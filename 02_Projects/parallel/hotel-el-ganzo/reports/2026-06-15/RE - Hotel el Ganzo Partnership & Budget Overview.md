## Project Overview

Parallel has been engaged by Hotel el Ganzo to design and install a permanent lighting system across multiple areas of the property. This is a full-service engagement: Parallel handles creative direction, technical design, purchasing, and installation.

### Scope of Work

| Area                | Description                                                             | Complexity |
| ------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------- |
| Jetty               | 30 RGBWA Thor pentawash uplights, palm tree uplight installation        | Low        |
| Rooftop             | LED strip lighting in channels across cabanas, restaurant, and bar roof | High       |
| Facade              | Continuous LED perimeter strips along top and bottom building borders   | High       |
| Rooftop Music Venue | Full lighting redesign to reduce ongoing gear rental dependency         | Medium     |
| New Restaurant      | Lighting design integrated with remodel                                 | High       |

## Salary Split Rationale

The salary split reflects the difference in workload and responsibility. Luca leads technical design, purchasing, and installation and carries full liability for the delivered system. The 50/50 owners draw maintains equal ownership of the business.

## Budget Structure

Total project budget: **$60,000–$80,000 USD / $1,030,800–$1,374,400 MXN**

Parallel purchases all equipment and manages the full budget. All gear is purchased at cost and billed to the client at cost + 15%. This markup is company revenue separate from the project profit bucket.

**IVA:** Final client invoices issued in Mexican Pesos will include an additional 16% IVA (Mexican VAT) required by law. This is collected from the client and remitted to the government — it does not affect Parallel's net earnings.

| Bucket | Allocation | Purpose |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Equipment | 35% | All gear, materials, and hardware (billed to client at cost + 15%) |
| Salaries | 35% | Luca and Alonso compensation |
| Gross Profit | 30% | Business profit before consultant fee |

**Note:** Roles are disclosed to the client. Salary amounts are internal.

## Key Agreements

- Salary split reflects role contribution (65% Luca / 35% Alonso).
- Owners draw is split equally (50/50) reflecting equal business ownership.
- Parallel handles all purchasing. All gear is billed to the client at cost + 15%. This fee is company revenue.
- The consultant fee ($4,500 USD / $77,310 MXN) is fixed and comes from project profit.
- Salary amounts are not disclosed to the client. Roles are.
- Both Luca and Alonso attend client proposal and render meetings. Alonso leads; Luca handles technical questions.
- Alonso is not required to attend installation days onsite or preproduction days.

## What Luca Is Not Expected To Do

The following responsibilities fall outside Luca's scope.

- **Client relationship management:** Luca is not the primary contact for Santiago or Alvaro. Day-to-day client communication is Alonso's responsibility.
- **Leading client meetings:** Alonso leads all meetings. Luca attends to answer technical questions only.
- **Financial tracking and payments:** Luca does not manage the project budget, track expenses, or execute payments. He assists when needed but does not own this.
- **Content creation and documentation:** Filming, photography coordination, social media, and portfolio management are Alonso's responsibility.
- **Business development:** Sourcing new clients and managing the commercial pipeline is Alonso's domain.

## What Alonso Is Not Expected To Do

The following responsibilities fall outside Alonso's scope. This is intentional — the 65/35 salary split reflects that Luca carries a significantly heavier operational load on these items.

- **Onsite installation:** Alonso is not required to be present during installation days or preproduction setup. This includes all physical rigging, cabling, DMX/ArtNet programming, and technical commissioning.
- **Vendor selection and order setup:** Alonso does not select vendors, negotiate gear pricing, or configure purchase orders. Luca owns all procurement decisions and hands off confirmed orders for payment.
- **Technical troubleshooting:** Any technical issues during or after installation are Luca's responsibility to diagnose and resolve.
- **Crew and contractor management:** Managing installation labor, subcontractors, and on-site logistics is Luca's domain.
- **Post-install maintenance:** Technical upkeep and any retainer service calls are handled by Luca.
