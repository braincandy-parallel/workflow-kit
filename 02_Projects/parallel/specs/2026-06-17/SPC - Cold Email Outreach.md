# SPC - Cold Email Outreach: Wedding Planner Directory

**Project:** Parallel
**Owner:** Alonso
**Goal:** Generate qualified planner inquiries from existing email directory
**Validated:** Yes — previous campaigns generated ~$20,000 USD in closed sales
**Contact list:** Existing directory of planner names and emails

---

## 1. Context

Cold email to planners has worked for Parallel before. This spec systematizes the approach so it can be run consistently and tracked properly. The list exists — the job is to work it methodically rather than sending one-off emails and losing track.

The tone is peer-to-peer, not sales pitch. Planners receive vendor solicitations constantly. The emails that work treat the planner as a professional sourcing the right tool for their client, not a prospect to be converted.

---

## 2. List Management

Before sending anything, organize the directory:

| Column | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| First name | For personalization |
| Last name | |
| Company / agency name | For personalization |
| Email | Primary send address |
| Market | Where they primarily plan (US, Canada, Mexico City, etc.) |
| Specialty | Destination weddings, corporate, local, etc. |
| Status | Untouched / Emailed / Replied / In conversation / Closed / Not interested |
| Last contact date | |
| Notes | Anything relevant from a conversation |

Sort by market and specialty before sending — planners who specifically do destination weddings in Los Cabos or Baja are first priority.

---

## 3. Sending Batches

Do not send to the entire list at once. Work in batches of 20-30 per week.

**Why batches:**
- Easier to track and follow up
- If a subject line isn't working, you catch it early and adjust before burning the whole list
- Avoids triggering spam filters from a sudden large send volume

**Priority order:**
1. Planners who specialize in Los Cabos / Baja / destination weddings in Mexico
2. US and Canadian planners who run destination wedding programs
3. Mexico City planners with destination wedding clients
4. All others

## 4. Email Templates

### Initial Email

**Subject line options** (test one per batch — track which gets better open rates):
```
Option A: Projection mapping + laser shows for Los Cabos events — Parallel
Option B: For your Los Cabos clients — lighting design + production
Option C: [First name] — working on any Los Cabos events this season?
```

**Body:**

```
Hi [First name],

I'm Luca, co-founder of Parallel — a lighting design and production studio based in Los Cabos. We work with planners on projection mapping, laser shows, custom LED installations, and full lighting design.

A few venues we've worked at: Acre, One&Only Palmilla, Viceroy, Chileno Bay, Amara, Hotel San Cristobal.

What makes us different from a standard production company: we design each event from scratch rather than pulling from a package list. If a client has a specific vision, we figure out how to build it.

If you have any Los Cabos clients coming up who might need something in this space, I'd be glad to be a resource. Happy to share more about what we do or get on a quick call if that's useful.

Our work: parallel-projects.net

Best,
Alonso

Parallel
parallel-projects.net
parallelprojectsmx@gmail.com
```

---

### Follow-Up Email (send 5-7 days after initial, if no reply)

**Subject line:** `Re: [original subject]` — keep it as a reply thread

**Body:**

```
Hi [First name],

Just following up on my note from last week in case it got buried.

If you have any upcoming Los Cabos events where lighting design or production is part of the conversation, I'm happy to be a resource — no pressure.

parallel-projects.net if you'd like to see recent work.

Luca
```

---

### Second Follow-Up (send 7-10 days after first follow-up, if no reply)

Send this only once. After this, mark as "not interested" and move on — do not contact again unless they reach out first.

**Body:**

```
Hi [First name],

Last note from me — I know inboxes are busy.

If a Los Cabos event comes up where you need lighting design, projection, or lasers, feel free to reach out. We'd be glad to help.

Luca — Parallel
parallel-projects.net
```

## 5. Personalization

The templates above are the base. Add one personalized line where you can — it meaningfully improves reply rates.

Sources for personalization:
- Their website: "I saw you work with [venue] regularly — we've done a few events there"
- A recent project: "Congrats on [wedding name] — saw it on your Instagram"
- A mutual connection: "I think you've worked with [person] — small world in Cabo"

One line is enough. Don't overdo it — it reads as research theater if there's too much.

---

## 6. Timing

- Send Tuesday through Thursday, between 9am and 11am in the recipient's time zone
- Avoid Mondays (inbox recovery day) and Fridays (winding down)
- Do not send during major US holidays or the week between Christmas and New Year

---

## 7. Tracking

Maintain a simple tracking spreadsheet (can live in Google Sheets):

| Name | Company | Email | Batch | Date sent | Follow-up 1 | Follow-up 2 | Status | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|-------|-----------|-------------|-------------|--------|-------|

**Status options:**
- `Untouched` — not yet contacted
- `Emailed` — initial sent, no reply
- `FU1 sent` — first follow-up sent
- `FU2 sent` — second follow-up sent
- `Replied` — any reply received
- `In conversation` — active back-and-forth
- `Meeting booked` — call or meeting scheduled
- `Closed` — project booked
- `Not interested` — explicitly declined or no response after full sequence
- `Unsubscribe` — asked to be removed, do not contact again

---

## 8. When Someone Replies

Reply within 24 hours. The goal of the first reply is to get on a call, not to close over email.

**If they express interest:**
- Thank them briefly
- Ask one qualifying question: "What type of events do you typically plan in Los Cabos — mostly weddings, or a mix?"
- Offer to jump on a quick call: "Happy to do a 15-minute call if that's easier — what does your schedule look like this week?"

**If they ask for pricing:**
- Don't quote rates cold over email — too many variables
- Respond: "Happy to give you a sense of ranges — it depends a lot on the specific event. A quick call would be the fastest way to give you a real number. Are you free this week?"

**If they say they're not interested:**
- Thank them, don't push, mark as "Not interested"
- Reply: "No problem at all — appreciate you getting back to me. Feel free to reach out if anything comes up down the line."

---

## 9. What to Measure

Track these after each batch:

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Open rate | 40%+ (subject line working) |
| Reply rate | 5-10% (content resonating) |
| Positive reply rate | 2-5% (qualified interest) |
| Meetings booked per 100 emails | 2-4 |

If open rate is below 40%, test a different subject line in the next batch.
If reply rate is below 5%, the body copy or targeting needs adjustment.
