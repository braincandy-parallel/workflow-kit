# SPC - Instagram Organic Content: @parallel_mx

**Project:** Parallel
**Owner:** Alonso
**Goal:** Build consistent Instagram presence using existing event archive and new coverage
**Account:** @parallel_mx
**Audience:** Wedding planners, event coordinators, venue managers in Los Cabos and destination wedding markets (US, Canada, Mexico City)

---

## 1. What This Account Is For

Instagram is Parallel's portfolio in motion. Planners who find Parallel through Google, referrals, or cold outreach will check the Instagram before reaching out. The account needs to look like an active, credible studio — not a dormant page.

The goal is not follower count. It is:
- Giving planners enough visual evidence to trust Parallel
- Showing up when planners search hashtags or locations
- Creating a library of content that can be linked in proposals and emails

---

## 2. Content Pillars

Every post fits into one of these categories:

| Pillar | What it is | Frequency |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Event coverage** | Photos or Reels from a past or recent event | 3x/week |
| **Behind the scenes** | Setup, rigging, gear prep, team at work | 1x/week |
| **Detail shots** | Close-up of a specific lighting effect, laser beam, LED installation | 1x/week |
| **Venue feature** | A space that Parallel has worked at — venue-focused framing | occasional |

Start with event coverage. The archive is deep enough to run for months without shooting anything new.

---

## 3. Posting Cadence

| Format | Frequency | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Feed posts (photos) | 3x/week | Mon, Wed, Fri |

3 feed posts per week is the target. Reels and Stories are optional — add them when footage is available, not on a forced schedule.

**Scheduling tool:** Meta Business Suite (free). Batch 2 weeks of posts at a time.

**Cross-posting:** Post to both Instagram and Facebook from Meta Business Suite. No Facebook-specific content — Instagram drives strategy, Facebook mirrors automatically.

---

## 4. Working Through the Archive

Parallel has a backlog of uncovered events. Process them systematically rather than posting at random.

### Archive workflow (do this once, then maintain):

1. List every event that has photos or footage but has never been posted
2. Sort by visual quality — start with the strongest events first
3. For each event, select 3-5 hero shots (the images that best represent what was done)
4. Write a caption for each (see Section 5)
5. Schedule using Meta Business Suite (free) or Later — batch 2 weeks at a time

A backlog of 10 events = 30-50 posts = 2-3 months of content without shooting anything new.

---

## 5. Caption Formula

Keep captions short and confident. Planners don't read paragraphs on Instagram.

**Structure:**
1. One line describing the visual concept or the moment (not the gear)
2. Venue name and event type (wedding, corporate, dinner, etc.)
3. Optional: one sentence on what made this event technically interesting
4. CTA: "Link in bio for inquiries" or "DM us to discuss your event"
5. 5-8 hashtags on the last line

**Example:**
```
Projection mapping across the full facade at golden hour.

Hotel San Cristobal — private wedding reception.

Built and operated live by our team. Content created specifically for the building's geometry.

Link in bio for inquiries.

#projectionmapping #loscabos #weddingproduction #eventlighting #cabowedding #lightingdesign #parallelmx
```

**Do not:**
- Write long paragraphs
- Use clichés ("magical night," "dream wedding," "once in a lifetime")
- Lead with gear specs
- Use more than 8 hashtags

## 7. Tagging Strategy

Tag the venue in every post where one is identifiable. Venues reshare posts that feature their space — free reach to their audience, which includes exactly the planners Parallel wants.

- Tag the venue account in the photo itself (not just the caption)
- Tag them in the caption: "📍 @acrebaja" or "📍 @oneandonly_palmilla"
- If a planner was involved and has an Instagram, tag them too

When a venue reshares: respond to the reshare with a thank you in Stories. This signals active engagement to both the venue and the algorithm.

---

## 8. Reels Format

Reels are the highest-reach format on Instagram. One Reel per week is enough.

**Format:**
- Length: 15-30 seconds (moving toward 30 seconds as primary target)
- Open with the action shot — no long intro, no fade-in logo
- Music: use Instagram's native music library at post time (avoids Content ID issues)
- No heavy editing needed — a clean sequence of 4-6 strong clips cuts itself

**Content ideas from the archive:**
- Event highlight (best 5-6 clips from a single event, cut to music)
- Transformation cut (venue before Parallel sets up vs. during event)
- Single effect featured (30 seconds of laser aerial effects from one event)
- Time-lapse of rigging/setup (if footage exists)

---

## 9. New Event Coverage

For every event going forward, capture:

| Shot | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Wide establishing shot | Shows the full environment and scale |
| Hero element close-up | The most visually impressive single element |
| Guest reaction (optional) | Only if it reads well — don't force it |
| Detail/texture shot | LED strip, laser beam, projection surface |
| BTS during setup | Rigging, programming screen, team working |

Shoot vertically (9:16) for Stories and Reels. Horizontal (4:5 or square) for feed photos. If only shooting one orientation, shoot vertical — it can be cropped for feed.

---

## 10. What Not to Post

- Generic motivational quotes
- Reposts of other studios' work
- Blurry or dark photos (better to skip an event than post poor quality)
- Gear-only shots with no event context
- Anything that looks like it was shot casually on a phone without intention

---

## 11. Monthly Review

At the end of each month, check:
- Which posts got the most reach and saves (saves are the highest-value signal)
- Which content pillar performed best
- Whether any venue or planner tagged Parallel (reshare those in Stories)
- Whether the DM inbox has any unread inquiries

Adjust the content mix based on what planners are saving and engaging with most.
